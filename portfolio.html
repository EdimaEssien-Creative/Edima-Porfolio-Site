About

I started my career in architecture — studying spatial systems, material behavior, and how people move through built environments. That foundation shapes everything I design today.

Now, as a creative technologist and product designer, I channel that spatial intuition into digital products. I'm driven by user research — the deep, messy, revelatory kind where you sit with users, watch them struggle, and let their reality reshape your assumptions.

My sweet spot is interaction design: the choreography of how interfaces respond, transition, and guide. Whether it's a coach scanning athlete data mid-race or an architect comparing façade systems worth millions, I design interactions that make complex decisions feel clear and considered.

User Research

Contextual inquiry, stakeholder interviews, diary studies, synthesis frameworks, journey mapping

Interaction Design

Micro-interactions, state management, gesture patterns, motion choreography, responsive behavior

Creative Technology

Spatial & Systems Design

05

Design Process

01

Research

Contextual inquiry, stakeholder interviews, and field observation. I go where users are — not where assumptions say they should be.

02

Synthesize

Affinity mapping, journey frameworks, and insight distillation. Turning raw research into a clear problem statement the whole team can rally around.

03

Prototype

Interaction-first design — from paper sketches to coded prototypes. I test interaction patterns early and refine the choreography of every state and transition.

04

Ship & Learn

Design QA, developer pairing, and post-launch usability testing. The work isn't done at handoff — it's done when real users validate it.
